My comments on the above:
- ***"even those not training models"***. The model is the basis of AI. So why would you not study them? 
- ***"they (programmers) think in code"***. What matters is you understand that AI does NOT think**. LLMs (TFs) are just deterministic binary code. 
- ***"all abstractions are leaky"***. AI is not an abstraction. It is binary computation / pattern recognition. AI only knows numbers. 
- ***"when AI produces suboptimal code"***. (???). Producing code is AI's primary strength. The problem is trying to get AI to deal with non-code data. 
- ***"the person who understands the substrate catches the failures everyone else misses"***. The failures of AI are obvious. You dont' catch them, they catch you. When your AI "FSD" tries to drive you into a lake, you notice. Understanding the fundamentals allows you to focus on AI projects that will work.
