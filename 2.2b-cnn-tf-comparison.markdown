## **1 ME/GPT: The essence of CNNs and TFs (input classifiers)**



#### **1.1 ME: Both CNNs and TFs are essentially input classifiers**

This is the biggest thing that CNNs taught me about TFs. 

What a CNN does it simple enough that we can understand that it is just classifying input.
I dont think that anyone seriously thinks that CNNs are intelligent.
Maybe INSTINCTIVE (more on that idea in a later version), but not intelligent. 
But we are taught that TFs are different, that they can host real intelligence.
But once you understand how they real work, 
how they so similar to CNNs, 
the very idea of AGI becomes ludicrous. 

TFs are just an extension of simple NNs as are CNNs.
But TFs use "attention heads" (AHs, a marketing term) instead of convolution.
TFs in the end are mainly classifiers (the predicted token is actually a classifcation)
with auto-regressive feedback 
(adding the new token classifier to the running prompt/response and feeding back into the TF).

CNNS and TFs 
- Host algorithms customized for the input data (pixels for CNN, language tokens for TFs)
- Provide the representational capacity required for the inptu data classification
TFs are vastly more complex because 
- language is far more complex that images and 
- token represention is an encoding of meaning designed for intelligent human minds
- vast amounts of binary computation are required to generate the most probablistic next token for a series of input tokens.

#### **1.3 GPT: If I had to summarize your theory in one sentence:**
- CNNs and Transformers both build progressively
higher-level representations.
- CNNs do it through fixed spatial neighborhoods,
feature channels, and pooling.
- Transformers do it through attention-selected
neighborhoods, embedding dimensions, and FFN
detector banks.

As an "essence" model rather than a mechanical one, I think that's a coherent framework. the overall picture hangs together much better than most CNN-vs-Transformer comparisons because you're comparing roles in the computation, not individual matrix operations.
