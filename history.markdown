ZiptieAI has evolved rapidly alongside major advances in technology.
What began as a drone-focused project expanded into a broader exploration of practical AI systems, software platforms, and modern engineering tools.

---

#### 1. [AI Drones](/aidrones/)

The original focus was intelligent drone systems.
At that time, AI assistance was limited compared with today. Typical AI capabilities centered on:

- Camera vision
- Object recognition using CNNs
- Autonomous flight support
- AI-generated control inputs that could modify flight plans

This phase included drone hardware builds, simulation, and flight-control integration.

---

#### 2. [Tech Stacks & Docs](/tech-stacks/)

The next phase shifted toward software systems and documentation.
It began with wikis and technical documentation, then expanded into websites and full-stack development. Modern AI tools dramatically accelerated the learning and build process.
Key areas included:

- Documentation systems
- Websites
- MERN stacks
- REST / GraphQL APIs
- Deployment workflows

The speed of development using modern AI assistants was a major turning point.

---

#### 3. [LLMs](/sandbox/)

Attention then shifted to the large language models transforming the software world.
Focus areas included:

- API-based model use
- Local and remote model deployment
- Small-model experimentation
- Fine-tuning research
- RAG
- MCP
- Tool-enabled AI systems

This phase centered on understanding how LLMs could be used in practical applications.

---

#### 4. [Robotic AI](/robotic-ai/)

As the field evolved, it became clear that future AI would involve more than prompt systems and model deployment.
Inspired by Yann LeCun and JEPA-style world models, research shifted toward AI for robotics:

- Representation learning
- Prediction-based systems
- Belief tracking
- Control loops
- Planning under uncertainty

Many concepts connected naturally with earlier drone work, including estimation and autonomy.

---

#### 5. [Agentic AI](/agentic-ai/)

The current focus is Agentic AI.
While robotics remains important, the most immediate opportunities appear in AI systems that combine language models with deterministic software, tools, workflows, and enterprise data.
A major inspiration is Palantir Technologies systems such as Maven-style platforms.
These architectures can be applied across many industries:

- Logistics
- Manufacturing
- Healthcare
- Defense
- Operations
- Business intelligence

This is likely to be one of the fastest-growing areas of AI in the near future.
